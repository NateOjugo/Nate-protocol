Your dopamine system — how it works
📵
No Phone — First 30 Min
DAILY · HARD RULE
Your dopamine system recalibrates overnight. Scrolling before it finishes resets the clock and trains your brain to need stimulation to function — this is the root of the scroll-then-unmotivated loop.
Charger goes across the room tonight. The friction is the point.
🚶
Neighborhood Walk = Morning Light Protocol
DAILY
Your walk does double duty: low-stimulation movement and circadian reset. Getting outside within 45 min of waking — no sunglasses — sets your body clock and determines when melatonin starts that night. Most reliable lever to pull your sleep schedule earlier within 2–3 weeks.
10 min minimum. Cloudy days count. No screen substitute works.
💨
Breathwork — Nervous System Reset
3×/WEEK
Breathwork is one of the fastest ways to shift from anxious/scattered to calm/focused without external stimulation. Box breathing or cyclic sighing (double inhale through nose, long exhale through mouth) before a deep work session primes your brain for the task instead of fighting it.
4–5 minutes is enough. Use it right before outreach when resistance is highest.
🏃
Cardio — Dopamine + BDNF Builder
3×/WEEK
Cardio — separate from lifting — produces a sustained dopamine and BDNF release that lasts hours after. This is your cognitive performance enhancer. Run before your outreach block when you can. Running outside also doubles as your morning walk.
20–30 min moderate intensity. Outside beats treadmill for circadian benefits.
🧘
Meditation — Procrastination Exit Ramp
3×/WEEK
When you're stuck, dopamine is in a trough. Cleaning or scrolling LinkedIn gives a tiny hit but doesn't reset the system. Instead, do something harder than sitting still — 10 min focusing on your breath when it's frustrating deepens the trough, forcing a faster rebound to motivated baseline.
The discomfort during meditation IS the mechanism. Staying with it is the rep.
📨
Outreach is Non-Negotiable
3×/WEEK · PRIORITY
Once or twice a week right now — goal is 3x minimum. Not research, not planning, not tracker-building. Sending actual messages to developers, GCs, and investor networks. That's the rep that compounds toward where you're going.
Treat it like gym. Don't wait to feel ready. Go, then feel ready.
⚠️
Don't Stack Dopamine
HARD RULE
Deleting TikTok and Instagram was right. Short-form video is the most aggressive dopamine spiker you encounter — it makes everything feel slow by comparison. Same principle applies to always stacking gym + music + pre-workout + caffeine. Occasionally do the hard thing with no external rewards to keep intrinsic motivation intact.
The goal: make effort itself feel like the reward. That's the holy grail.
